INFORMATION COUNTER

  • There are two types of blood donation — whole blood and apheresis.

  • The whole blood donation takes 30 minutes and can only be done once in three months

  • Apheresis donation takes over an hour and can only be done at the centre.


  • Altruism is the top reason driving Malaysians to donate blood.

  • Benefits to being a blood donor including free Hepatitis B vaccine shot, free outpatient treatment and free health screenings according to donation frequency.

  • Four to five hundred units of blood are needed daily, one unit is about 450ml of blood.

  • One blood donation can save three lives.

  • There are three different components in blood: red blood cell, platelets and plasma.

  • Blood type “A” tends to run short.

  • Those aged below 18 must get parental consent.
  • There is no age limit in organ donation, and there is no discrimination between gender, race and religion.

  • We can also donate tissues such as corneas, skin, bones and heart valves.

  • There are two types of donors – living and cadaveric donors.

  • In August 2018, Malaysia’s cadaveric organ donation rate was between 0.4 and 0.6 per million population (pmp).

  • several reasons for reluctance to donate included fear of organs being used for research, a desire to be buried whole, and fear of less effective treatment if the patient is known to be a donor.

  • In Malaysia, the family has the final say for organs to be donated though the deceased pledged to be a cadaveric donor.
